[Question]
A particle moves along the positive $x$-axis. Its velocity $v$ as a function of position $x$ is continuously given by the equation: $$v(x) = \frac{\alpha}{x}$$ where $\alpha$ is a positive constant. Assuming the particle is located at $x = x_0$ at time $t = 0$, which of the following expressions correctly represents the acceleration of the particle as a function of position $x$?

[Derivation]:
Using the calculus chain rule for acceleration $a = v \frac{dv}{dx}$:
1. Find the derivative: $\frac{dv}{dx} = -\frac{\alpha}{x^2}$
2. Multiply by $v$: $a = \left(\frac{\alpha}{x}\right) \left(-\frac{\alpha}{x^2}\right) = -\frac{\alpha^2}{x^3}$
